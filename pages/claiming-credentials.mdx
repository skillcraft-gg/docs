# Claiming credentials

Claims turn evidence into portable signals you can share with collaborators, hiring teams, or automation systems.

## Typical flow

1. Generate evidence through normal project activity.
2. Group related items into a claim bundle.
3. Sign and store the resulting credential artifact.
4. Publish the claim for consumers.

## Best practices

- Keep claim scopes narrow (one credential at a time).
- Include enough context for reviewers to reproduce checks.
- Link every claim to an immutable commit or registry entry.

## CLI example

```bash
skillcraft evidence status
skillcraft claim add --skill="skillcraft-gg/rapid-delivery" --level=proven
skillcraft claim publish
```

## Share with teams

- Keep claim notes short and outcome-focused.
- Store proof references in the same location as the credential.
- Rotate credential names when scope changes.
